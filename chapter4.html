Chapter 4

Replaced

"Nonsense!" Engineer Bob was mad! "Charlie is still full of zip and zowie! I will telegraph the head office of the Mid-World Railway Company! I will telegraph the president, Mr. Raymond Martin, myself! I know him because he once gave me a Good Service Award, and afterwards Charlie and I took his little daughter for a ride. I let her pull the lanyard, and Charlie whistled his loudest for her!"

"I am sorry, Bob," said Mr. Briggs. "It was Mr. Martin himself who ordered the new diesel train."

It was true. And so Charlie the Choo-Choo was shunted off to a short track in the farthest corner of Mid-World's St. Louis yard.

Now the HONNNK! HONNNK! of the Burlington Zephyr was heard on the St. Louis to Topeka route, and Charlie's whistle blew no more.

A family of mice nested in the seat where Engineer Bob once sat so proudly, watching the countryside speed past; a family of swallows nested in his smokestack.

Charlie was lonely and very sad. He missed the steel tracks and bright blue skies and wide open spaces. Sometimes, late at night, he thought of these things and cried dark, oily tears.

This rusted his fine Stratham headlight which was now always dark.
